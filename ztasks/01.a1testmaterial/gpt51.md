Below is a complete, app-ready lesson plan and learning journey for Spanish A1 (CEFR A1). It covers the core competences that appear on A1 tests, a modular curriculum with lessons/practice/quizzes, demo content for several lessons, and multiple simulated A1 test samples (reading, listening, writing, speaking) together with answers and scoring guidance you can use for in-app drills and mock exams.

1) Overview — what A1 tests and core things to learn
- What A1 measures:
  - Basic communicative ability in everyday situations: understand and use familiar everyday expressions and very simple phrases aimed at satisfying concrete needs.
  - Can introduce themselves and others and can ask and answer questions about personal details (e.g., where they live, people they know, things they have).
  - Can interact in a simple way provided the other person talks slowly and clearly and is prepared to help.
- Main skill areas tested:
  - Listening: understanding short utterances, questions, and statements about immediate concrete needs (e.g., numbers, times, places, simple directions).
  - Reading: understanding very short, simple texts (signs, timetables, short notices, personal emails).
  - Writing: writing simple phrases and sentences, short messages, completing forms with personal details.
  - Speaking: short dialogues/monologues: personal information, daily routine, likes/dislikes, asking for and giving simple information.
- Core grammar & vocabulary at A1:
  - Pronouns: yo, tú, él/ella/usted, nosotros/as, vosotros/as (optional), ellos/ellas/ustedes.
  - Present indicative of regular verbs (-ar, -er, -ir) and common irregulars: ser, estar, tener, ir, hacer, poder, decir (basic uses).
  - Basic question words: qué, quién, cómo, cuándo, dónde, por qué, cuál.
  - Simple negation: no + verb, nunca, nada, nadie.
  - Numbers (0–100, basic arithmetic), dates, days, months, telling time.
  - Basic adjectives and agreement (gender/number), comparatives simple: más/menos + adj.
  - Possessives: mi, tu, su, nuestro.
  - Demonstratives: este/esta/estos/estas, ese/aquél (basic).
  - Prepositions: en, a, con, de, para, por, desde, hasta.
  - Common everyday vocabulary: family, home, food, shopping, clothes, weather, transport, jobs, hobbies.
  - Polite phrases and simple connectors: y, pero, porque, también, o.
- Functional tasks typical for A1:
  - Introduce self/others, ask and answer personal questions.
  - Give basic directions and location information.
  - Make simple purchases, order food/drinks.
  - Fill out basic forms (name, address, nationality).
  - Understand short announcements/instructions.

2) Learning journeys — curriculum structure (12-week sample)
Design as progressive modules with lessons, micro-practices, quizzes, speaking labs, and periodic mock tests.

High-level structure (12 weeks, 3 lessons per week; each lesson ~30–45 min):
- Module 1: Foundations (Weeks 1–2)
  - Topic: Alphabet, pronunciation, greetings, basic phrases, numbers 0–30, present tense regular verbs, ser vs. estar.
- Module 2: Personal Info & Daily Life (Weeks 3–4)
  - Topic: Family, describing people, days/months/time, telling time, present tense irregulars (tener, ir), basic adjectives, possessives.
- Module 3: Home & Shopping (Weeks 5–6)
  - Topic: Rooms, furniture, shopping dialogues, articles and gender/number agreement, demonstratives, prepositions of place.
- Module 4: Food & Ordering (Weeks 7–8)
  - Topic: Food vocabulary, ordering in cafés/restaurants, numbers 0–100, prices, simple verbs pedir/comer/beber, expressions of likes/dislikes (gustar).
- Module 5: Travel & Directions (Weeks 9–10)
  - Topic: Transport, asking for directions, simple imperatives, location prepositions, timetables.
- Module 6: Review & Exam Preparation (Weeks 11–12)
  - Topic: Consolidation, timed practice tests, targeted drills for weak points, speaking mock tests.

Each lesson:
- Objective (1–2 short measurable goals).
- Input: short explanation + examples (grammar/vocab).
- Controlled practice: drills, gap-fill, matching.
- Production: short writing/speaking task.
- Mini-quiz: 5–8 items (auto-graded where possible).
- Flashcards + spaced repetition set (vocab items from the lesson).
- Optional listening clip + transcript.

Weekly practice elements:
- Pronunciation practice (short phrases with model audio).
- Mini-dialogues for roleplay.
- 1 listening exercise (30–90 seconds).
- 1 short writing task (20–50 words).
- 1 speaking prompt for recording (30–60 seconds).

3) Detailed lesson examples (demo content) — three full demo lessons

Lesson A: Greetings, Alphabet, Numbers 0–20, Presenting Yourself
- Objective:
  - Students can greet, say goodbye, introduce themselves (name, nationality), and say age.
  - Recognize and pronounce Spanish alphabet letters and numbers 0–20.
- Input (core phrases and grammar):
  - Saludos: Hola, Buenos días, Buenas tardes, Buenas noches; ¿Cómo estás? / ¿Cómo está usted?
  - Presentación: Me llamo Ana. Yo soy Ana. Soy de México. Tengo 25 años.
  - Alphabet note: letters with examples: a (amigo), b (bebé) — (link audio).
  - Numbers: cero, uno, dos, tres, cuatro, cinco, seis, siete, ocho, nueve, diez, once, doce, trece, catorce, quince, dieciséis, diecisiete, dieciocho, diecinueve, veinte.
- Controlled practice:
  - Match: English → Spanish (Hello → Hola; Goodbye → Adiós; Thank you → Gracias).
  - Fill-in: Complete the sentence: Me llamo _____.
  - Numbers drill: Type the Spanish word for 15: _____
- Production:
  - Write 3 sentences: name, nationality, age. Example model: Me llamo Carlos. Soy de España. Tengo 30 años.
  - Speaking: Record a 20-second self-introduction (name, country, age).
- Mini-quiz (5 items, auto-grade):
  1. Translate: "Good morning" → ? (Buenos días)
  2. Fill: "Me _____ Ana." (llamo)
  3. What is "seven"? (siete)
  4. Select formal "How are you?" → ¿Cómo está usted?
  5. Translate: "I am from Colombia." → Soy de Colombia.
- Feedback model:
  - Provide corrections for common errors (Me llamo vs. Mi nombre es; Tengo 25 años vs. Estoy 25 — incorrect).
- Flashcards: 15 items (greetings + numbers + Me llamo/soy/tengo).

Lesson B: Ser vs. Estar (basic uses), Adjectives (nationalities), Family Members
- Objective:
  - Use simple sentences with ser and estar for identity and temporary state; describe family members and nationalities with gender agreement.
- Input:
  - Ser (identity/origin/profession): Yo soy estudiante. Tú eres médico. Ella es mexicana.
  - Estar (location/health/temporary): Estoy cansado. La casa está en Madrid.
  - Nationalities: mexicano/mexicana, español/española, colombiano/colombiana.
  - Family vocab: madre, padre, hermano, hermana, hijo, hija, abuelo, abuela.
- Controlled practice:
  - Choose ser or estar: "Yo ___ profesor." → soy.
  - Gender agreement: Translate "She is Spanish" → Ella es española.
  - Match family words to images.
- Production:
  - Write 4 sentences about your family (names, relationship, nationality).
  - Speaking: Describe one family member in 30 seconds.
- Mini-quiz (6 items):
  - Fill: Ella ___ en la oficina. (está)
  - Translate: "My father is a doctor." → Mi padre es doctor.
  - Choose: Nationality masculine of "Argentina" → argentino
- Feedback and common mistakes explained.

Lesson C: Restaurant roleplay — ordering food (gustar + basic menu)
- Objective:
  - Order a drink and a meal in Spanish; use gustar to state likes/dislikes.
- Input:
  - Useful phrases: Quisiera..., Para mí..., ¿Me trae...? La cuenta, por favor.
  - Gustar: Me gusta el café. No me gusta el pescado. Me gustan las frutas.
  - Menu vocab: café, té, agua, pan, ensalada, pollo, pescado, arroz, postre.
- Controlled practice:
  - Construct: "I would like a coffee" → Quisiera un café.
  - Select: "I like fish" → Me gusta el pescado.
- Production:
  - Write a 2-line order for a café (drink + main).
  - Speaking roleplay: Student plays customer; app plays waiter (scripted) — record 30–45 sec.
- Mini-quiz (5):
  - Translate: "The bill, please." → La cuenta, por favor.
  - Choose correct: "I like apples." → Me gustan las manzanas.
- Feedback: Correct verb agreement with gustar (me gusta vs. me gustan).

4) Quizzes and spaced practice design
- Micro-quizzes (end of each lesson): 5–8 auto-graded items (multiple choice, fill-in).
- Weekly cumulative quizzes: 15–20 items covering vocabulary, grammar, short reading and listening items.
- Speaking evaluation: rubric-based scoring (see below).
- Flashcard SRS: each new vocab added to spaced repetition queue; review reminders.
- Error tracking: store common error types (e.g., ser/estar misuse, adjective agreement, verb endings) to generate targeted drills.

5) Speaking & Listening practice implementations
- Speaking:
  - Dialog simulations with branching responses; scaffolded prompts (first: read model; then record; then free reply).
  - Rubric for A1 speaking (0–5 scale per criterion): Pronunciation (0–5), Fluency (0–5), Grammar/accuracy (0–5), Task completion (0–5). Passing threshold e.g., average ≥3.
  - Instant feedback: highlight wrong verb forms, missing words; show model answers.
- Listening:
  - Short clips (20–90s) at slow natural speed. Provide transcript only after attempt to avoid reading.
  - Comprehension tasks: multiple choice, true/false, gap-fill.
  - Tip: include number understanding, times, prices, simple directions.

6) Simulated A1 Tests — samples with answer keys and scoring

Simulated A1 Test 1 — Short format (90 minutes total equivalent)
- Listening (25 points)
  - Part 1: 5 short dialogues; for each choose correct option (5 items).
  - Part 2: 1 short announcement/notice; answer 5 T/F or multiple choice (5 items).
  - Part 3: 5 questions based on short monologues (5 items).
  - Example listening item (transcript given here for app use; during mock test hide transcript and use audio):
    - Dialogue (customer & waiter):
      Waiter: Buenas tardes. ¿Qué desea?
      Customer: Quisiera un bocadillo de jamón y un café con leche, por favor.
      Waiter: ¿Algo más?
      Customer: No, gracias.
    - Question: ¿Qué quiere el cliente? (A) Un bocadillo de queso. (B) Un bocadillo de jamón y un café. (C) Solo un café.
    - Answer: (B)
- Reading (25 points)
  - Part 1: Match signs and short notices to meanings (10 items).
  - Part 2: Read a short email (50–70 words) and answer 5 multiple-choice questions (5 items).
  - Example reading item:
    - Text: "Hola Marta, soy Ana. Vivo en Sevilla. Trabajo en un hotel. ¿Vienes el sábado? Tenemos una fiesta a las ocho. Besos, Ana."
    - Q: ¿Dónde vive Ana? → Sevilla.
- Writing (20 points)
  - Task 1 (10 pts): Fill a short form: name, nationality, date of birth, address (auto-graded exact match on key fields).
  - Task 2 (10 pts): Write 25–40 words: introduce yourself and say where you live and what you do. Scoring rubric:
    - Task completion (4): all required info present.
    - Grammar/accuracy (3): basic grammar correct (ser/tener).
    - Vocabulary/Coherence (3): appropriate vocabulary and understandable.
  - Example prompt: "Escribe 3-4 frases sobre ti: nombre, país, profesión o estudio."
  - Model answer: "Me llamo Pedro. Soy de Perú. Vivo en Lima. Soy estudiante." (Full credit).
- Speaking (30 points)
  - Task 1 (10 pts): Answer 3 personal questions (name, where from, what you do) — 1–2 sentences each.
  - Task 2 (10 pts): Roleplay ordering in a café (student plays customer).
  - Task 3 (10 pts): Describe a photo (simple) — e.g., family at home, 30–40 seconds.
  - Speaking rubric (per task): Pronunciation (3), Fluency (3), Grammar/Accuracy (4). Passing often requires minimum overall >= 60%.

Rubrics — scoring guidance (for in-app auto-evaluation + teacher override)
- Listening & Reading: correct/incorrect, weight items to sum to target points.
- Writing: auto-check for required tokens (name, country, profession) + approximate grammar scoring; show suggestions.
- Speaking: automated scoring can use ASR to match tokens and pronunciation scoring. Provide rubric:
  - Pronunciation: 0–3 (0 = incomprehensible, 3 = good intelligibility).
  - Fluency: 0–3 (0 = long pauses, 3 = fluent short speech).
  - Accuracy: 0–4 (0 = many errors, 4 = mostly accurate).
  - Task completion: 0–2 (0 = missing required info, 2 = all present).
  - Example passing threshold per task: ≥50% of max points; overall passing: ≥60% total.

10) Implementation notes for app engineers/content creators
- Keep listening at slow natural pace (approx. 70–85% of native speed).
- Provide transcripts only after student attempts.
- Accept spelling variants for names and simple tokens; normalize diacritics optionally.
- For auto-grading free text: use token presence + simple grammar heuristics. Flag partial matches for teacher review.
- For speaking ASR: focus on detecting key tokens (name, country, verbs) and measuring pause length rather than exact grammar.
