### **Spanish A1 Learning App: Comprehensive Plan**

**Goal:** Equip users with the necessary knowledge and skills to confidently pass the Spanish A1 proficiency test by offering structured lessons, interactive practice, quizzes, and simulated test experiences.

---

#### **1. Core A1 Topics & Test Content**

The A1 level focuses on very basic language use, enabling a learner to understand and use familiar everyday expressions and very basic phrases.

**Core Learning Areas:**

1.  **Self & Others:**
    *   **Greetings & Farewells:** *Hola, Adiós, Buenos días/tardes/noches.*
    *   **Introductions:** *Me llamo..., Soy de..., ¿Cómo te llamas?*
    *   **Personal Information:** Name, nationality, age, profession, where you live.
    *   **Family:** Basic vocabulary (*madre, padre, hermano, hermana*).
    *   **Describing People:** Basic adjectives (*alto, bajo, guapo, simpático*).
2.  **Daily Life:**
    *   **Numbers:** 0-100 (at least 0-20 for core A1, 0-100 for practical use).
    *   **Colors:** *rojo, azul, verde, amarillo.*
    *   **Time & Days:** *hoy, mañana, lunes, martes, ¿Qué hora es?*
    *   **Food & Drink:** Basic vocabulary (*agua, café, pan, fruta*).
    *   **Basic Objects:** Common classroom/household items (*libro, mesa, silla*).
    *   **Places:** Basic locations (*casa, escuela, tienda, restaurante*).
3.  **Basic Communication:**
    *   **Asking & Giving Directions:** Simple phrases (*¿Dónde está...?, aquí, allí*).
    *   **Making Requests:** *Por favor, ¿Puedo tener...?, Quiero...*
    *   **Expressing Likes/Dislikes:** *Me gusta/no me gusta.*
    *   **Shopping:** Asking prices (*¿Cuánto cuesta?*).
4.  **Fundamental Grammar:**
    *   **Nouns & Articles:** Gender (masculine/feminine), number (singular/plural), definite (*el, la, los, las*) and indefinite (*un, una, unos, unas*) articles.
    *   **Adjectives:** Agreement in gender and number.
    *   **Verbs (Present Tense - Regular):** -AR, -ER, -IR verbs.
    *   **Key Irregular Verbs (Present Tense):**
        *   **Ser** (to be - permanent characteristics, origin, time, profession).
        *   **Estar** (to be - temporary states, location, health, feelings).
        *   **Tener** (to have).
        *   **Ir** (to go).
        *   **Haber** (hay - there is/are).
    *   **Question Words:** *¿Qué?, ¿Quién?, ¿Cuándo?, ¿Dónde?, ¿Cómo?, ¿Por qué?, ¿Cuánto?*
    *   **Prepositions of Place:** *en, a, de, con, sin.*
    *   **Personal Pronouns:** *yo, tú, él/ella/usted, nosotros/as, vosotros/as, ellos/ellas/ustedes.*
    *   **Possessive Adjectives:** *mi, tu, su.*

**What will be tested on A1?**

A typical A1 test assesses the four core language skills: Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking, all at a very basic level.

*   **Listening (Comprensión Auditiva):**
    *   Understanding single words, very short, simple sentences, and common phrases in slow, clear speech (e.g., greetings, personal information, numbers, simple requests).
    *   Identifying main ideas in very short, basic conversations.
*   **Reading (Comprensión de Lectura):**
    *   Understanding very short, simple texts (e.g., signs, notices, simple postcards, short personal messages, forms).
    *   Extracting specific information (e.g., names, numbers, prices, times).
*   **Writing (Expresión Escrita):**
    *   Filling in basic forms with personal details.
    *   Writing very short, simple messages or postcards (e.g., 20-30 words) about familiar topics.
    *   Constructing simple sentences using basic grammar and vocabulary.
*   **Speaking (Expresión Oral):**
    *   Introducing oneself and others.
    *   Asking and answering simple personal questions (e.g., name, origin, age, profession, hobbies).
    *   Participating in very basic, routine conversations (e.g., ordering food, asking for directions).
    *   Describing basic objects or situations with single words or simple phrases.

#### **2. Learning Journeys: Structure & Content**

The app should organize content into modules or units, each containing lessons, practice, and quizzes.

**Structure Example (Module-based):**

*   **Module 1: Getting Started**
    *   Lesson 1.1: Greetings & Introductions
    *   Lesson 1.2: The Alphabet & Pronunciation
    *   Lesson 1.3: Personal Pronouns & Verb "Ser" (to be)
    *   Lesson 1.4: Numbers 0-10
    *   Module 1 Quiz
*   **Module 2: Describing Yourself & Others**
    *   Lesson 2.1: Nouns & Articles (Gender/Number)
    *   Lesson 2.2: Adjectives & Agreement
    *   Lesson 2.3: Verb "Estar" (to be) & Locations
    *   Lesson 2.4: Family & Descriptions
    *   Module 2 Quiz
*   **Module 3: Daily Life & Routines**
    *   Lesson 3.1: Regular -AR Verbs (Present Tense)
    *   Lesson 3.2: Regular -ER/-IR Verbs (Present Tense)
    *   Lesson 3.3: Time & Days of the Week
    *   Lesson 3.4: Food & Drink Vocabulary
    *   Module 3 Quiz
*   ...and so on, covering all core A1 topics.

**Each Lesson Should Include:**

*   **Clear Explanations:** Grammar rules, vocabulary lists with audio pronunciation.
*   **Examples:** Contextual sentences.
*   **"Key Phrases" / "Survival Spanish" sections:** Immediately useful expressions.

**Practice Activities:**

*   **Vocabulary Drills:** Flashcards, matching, listen-and-repeat.
*   **Grammar Exercises:** Fill-in-the-blanks, sentence construction, multiple-choice.
*   **Listening Comprehension:** Short dialogues with questions.
*   **Reading Comprehension:** Short texts with questions.
*   **Speaking Prompts:** Record your answer, compare with native speaker audio (e.g., "Introduce yourself," "Describe your house").
*   **Writing Prompts:** Fill in a form, write a short message.

**Quizzes:**

*   Short, focused assessments after each lesson or module.
*   Mix of question types: multiple choice, true/false, fill-in-the-blanks, simple translations.
*   Immediate feedback and explanations for incorrect answers.

#### **3. Demo Content Examples**

**A. Lesson: Greetings & Introductions**

**Topic:** Basic Spanish Greetings and Self-Introduction
**Vocabulary:**
*   *Hola* (Hello)
*   *Buenos días* (Good morning/day)
*   *Buenas tardes* (Good afternoon/evening)
*   *Buenas noches* (Good evening/night)
*   *Adiós* (Goodbye)
*   *Hasta luego* (See you later)
*   *Me llamo...* (My name is...)
*   *Soy de...* (I am from...)
*   *¿Cómo te llamas?* (What's your name? - informal)
*   *¿De dónde eres?* (Where are you from? - informal)
*   *Encantado/a* (Nice to meet you - masculine/feminine)
*   *Mucho gusto* (Nice to meet you - general)
*   *¿Qué tal?* (How are you? / How's it going?)
*   *Bien, gracias.* (Good, thank you.)
*   *¿Y tú?* (And you? - informal)

**Explanation:**
In Spanish, greetings change depending on the time of day. "Hola" is general. "Buenos días" is used in the morning, "Buenas tardes" in the afternoon/early evening, and "Buenas noches" in the late evening/night.
To introduce yourself, you use *Me llamo [Your Name]* or *Soy [Your Name]*. To state your origin, use *Soy de [Country/City]*.
*Encantado* (if you are male) or *Encantada* (if you are female) means "nice to meet you." *Mucho gusto* is a gender-neutral alternative.

**Example Dialogue:**
Juan: *¡Hola! Buenos días. Me llamo Juan.*
Ana: *¡Hola Juan! Buenos días. Yo me llamo Ana. Encantada.*
Juan: *Mucho gusto, Ana. ¿De dónde eres?*
Ana: *Soy de España. ¿Y tú?*
Juan: *Soy de México.*
Ana: *¡Qué bien! Hasta luego.*
Juan: *Adiós, Ana.*
